My scores on JB Peterson's The Big Five Aspect Scale are as followed (in percentiles): Aggreableness, High (85th); Compassion, Moderately High (66th); Politeness, Very High (92nd); Conscientiousness, Moderately Low (35th); Industriousness, Moderately Low (27th); Orderliness, Typical or Average (48th); Extraversion, Moderately Low (31st); Enthusiasm, Moderately Low (30th); Assertiveness, Moderately Low (36th); Neuroticism, Typical or Average (53rd); Withdrawal, High (83rd); Volatility, Low (21st); Openness to Experience, Very High (94th); Intellect, High (86th); Openness, Very High (93rd). Ultimately, I have conflicting feelings regarding trait theory. This theory supposes that every person has a fundamental nature that can be roughtly partitioned into categories. As appealing as it is to believe that personality can be distilled down to a finite number of qualities, this ideology falls victim to the simple fact of animal growth. As people grow, and as a byproduct so does humanity, their being continues to redefine itself. So from one moment to the next, one's 'balance of traits' will inevitably be dissimilar. Not only will they differ, but new 'human traits' may arise from never-before-seen cultural movements while others may disappear as ideas and beliefs fall out of fashion. For this reason, I find these personality profiles to be rather benign. But I took one anyway. Enjoy!
