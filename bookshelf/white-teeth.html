David's Reading Journal

White Teeth

by Zadie Smith

finished Tuesday, December 27, 2016

I took a steady march through White Teeth. I started sometime in the middle of my last semester as an undergraduate student, unwillingly but regularly pushing it aside for pressing academic deadlines. I finally finished the novel during a family-and-friends road trip down in the Deep South. During a sunny noontime, at a rest stop in the middle of South Carolina, I closed the back flap and then group texted the friends who had recommended the book. It had been ages since I had been so drawn into a piece of fiction, so I felt obliged to thank them.

I am sure that the praise for Zadie Smith is boundless, so I will not waste any time trying to preface my adulation of her. Instead, I will just talk about what I most loved about this work.

The biggest draw-in for me was Zadie Smith's writing style. Her voice was an amalgamation of a youthful cant and classical eloquence. I never imagined using all-caps'd, mispelled profanities could come off as anything besides edgy in all the wrong ways. But she unironically uses words like "BITCHEZ," without undermining the novel's sophistication. I am grateful that White Teeth reached my life. After four years of awfully technical reading, it gave me a much-needed mental slap that reminded me how much fun reading can be.
